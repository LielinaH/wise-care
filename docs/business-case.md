# Business Case: Wise Care Navigation Platform

Wise Care solves a multi-stakeholder problem in the U.S. healthcare system: **the high operational friction of matching and entry into mental health care.** By positioning itself as a pure navigation and preparation layer, Wise Care creates economic value without taking on clinical liabilities or offering diagnostic services.

---

## 1. Stakeholder Value Propositions

### A. Individuals / Patients
- **Value:** Reduces search anxiety, clarifies insurance coverage, prepares clinician briefs, and secures direct connections.
- **Impact:** Speeds up time-to-appointment and decreases rate of drop-off.

### B. Providers / Clinics
- **Value:** Receives structured, pre-prepared intake briefs containing timelines and goals.
- **Impact:** Decreases clinician administrative burdens, lowers no-show rates, and speeds up time to therapeutic alignment.

### C. Employers & Universities
- **Value:** Offers a simple, private starting point for employees/students seeking support.
- **Impact:** Boosts benefit utilization (EAPs), decreases absenteeism, and provides aggregate insights on systemic access barriers (e.g. out-of-pocket costs, waitlists) without violating privacy.

### D. Insurers / Public Payers
- **Value:** Guides members to appropriate in-network care routes before conditions escalate to emergency rooms.
- **Impact:** Reduces overall claim costs and provides data on coverage shortages or regional waitlists.

---

## 2. Monetization Models (Ethical Guidelines)
Wise Care operates on a strict **privacy-first monetization policy**. *We do not sell, share, or monetize user health data, search parameters, or diagnostic briefs.*

Our revenue channels include:

### A. Enterprise B2B SaaS (Employers & Universities)
- **Model:** Per-Member-Per-Month (PMPM) access fee paid by the institution.
- **Why it works:** Institutions pay to improve employee/student well-being and EAP benefit utilization.

### B. Provider Network / Referral Software Integration
- **Model:** Software integration subscriptions for clinic groups.
- **Why it works:** Clinics pay to receive structured referrals and auto-verify insurance inputs, reducing clinic intake coordinator hours.

### C. Insurer Care Navigation Partnership
- **Model:** Outcome-based pilot programs targeting reduced ER visits.
- **Why it works:** Payers pay for navigation tools that successfully guide members to community groups, outpatient counseling, or medication management.
